      Entropy and the Second Law

      • Entropy. Gives an introduction to entropy based on N indpendent quantum harmonic oscillators. Based on Chapter 4 as well as my own perspective.
      • The microcanonical ensemble. Shows how the relation between temperature cand energy an be found by finding the entropy in a concrete example of N independent quantum oscillators. We will find the temperature by directly counting the number of ways to share the energy.
      • The Boltmann factor. Derives the Boltzmann factor from notions of entropy. This is based on 4.3 and 4.4. This completes the connection between the microcanonical ensemble (i.e. directy counting of ways to share the energy) and the canonical ensemble (i.e. using the Boltzman factor and partition functions).
      • Entropy of Ideal Mono-Atomic Gas. Computes the entropy of a classical mono-atomic gas, by directly counting microstates.
      • Entropy and Heat Flow. Works through several examples of entropy associated with heat flow. Readings: Chapter 14.1, 14.2
      • Entropy, Pressure, Mechanical Equilibrium. Discusses mechanical equilibrium (equalities of pressures), in equilibrium. Readings: Chapter 14.3
      • Entropy Change in free expansion. Works through entropy change of a free expansion. Readings: Chapter 14.4
      • Entropy of mixing. Entropy change due to mixing of two gasses. Readings: Chapter 14.6
      • Entropy and Carnot Efficiency. Discusses Carnot efficiency using entropy concepts. Shows that in a non-equilibrium process the entropy produced is greater than dQ/T.
      • Entropy of ideal gas from a mathematical perspective
        • Exact and Inexact Differentials, Blundell, appendix C.7 and Example 11.1
        • Heat and Entropy as inexact and exact differentials, This allows us to revisit the Carnot Cycle.
