Problem 2.   Estimates of Entropy

The Sackur-Tetrode equation which determines the entropy of an ideal mono-atomic gas:

S=N⁢kB⁢[log⁡(vNλth3)+52] (1)

where vN=V/N and λth is the thermal de Broglie wavelength. You should not have to look up numbers here.

  2. (b)

  3. (c)

    Sackur-Tetrode equation says

    S=N⁢kB⁢[log⁡(vNλth3)+52] (2)

    Thus, up to a logarithm (which is never very large), the entropy is of order N⁢kB. This gives a simple way to estimate entropy of any substance. It is of order the number of particles (times kB). Determine S/(N⁢kB) for Helium gas at STP.

Problem 3.   Degeneracy

Often there may be more than one quantum mechanical state with the same energy level Eℓ – this is known as degeneracy. A very common source of degeneracy is the quantum mechanical spin. If a particle has spin 12, then it can be in two quantum mechanical spin states, spin up and spin down. If the energy is independent of the spin, then there will be two quantum mechanical states with the same energy level Eℓ (one with spin up and one with spin down), and the degeneracy of the energy level is two, gℓ=2. In the partition function we sum over states, which is clearly related to the sum over energy levels as follows:

∑statese-Es/k⁢T=∑energy-levels,Eℓgℓ⁢e-Eℓ/k⁢T (3)

where gℓ is the degeneracy of the ℓ-th energy level.
