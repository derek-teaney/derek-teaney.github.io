1.5.1 Change of variables in 1d

Given a variable x and its probability distribution

d⁢𝒫x=P⁢(x)⁢d⁢x (1.52)

How do we find the probability of u where given a map φ:x→u, i.e. u⁢(x) is a function of x? Assuming that that the map is one to one we have

d⁢𝒫u=P⁢(x⁢(u))⁢|d⁢xd⁢u|⁢d⁢u=P⁢(x⁢(u))⁢d⁢u|d⁢ud⁢x| (1.53)

We note the absolute values here, which reflects the fact that when discussing probability we are asking wether a particle is in a bin of size d⁢u, and the bin size isn’t like positive or negative.

The general formula for change of variables is

∫Rf⁢(x)⁢dx=∫Ruf⁢(x⁢(u))⁢|d⁢xd⁢u|⁢du (1.54)

Here R is the region of integration for x (for example R is x∈[1,2]), and Ru is the image of R under the map, e.g. if the map is u⁢(x)=-2⁢x then Ru is [-4,-2]. To set yourself straight of this notation just make the change of variables, getting the signs right:

I=∫[1,2]dx=∫12dx=∫[-4,-2]12⁢du=∫-4-212⁢du=1. (1.55)
