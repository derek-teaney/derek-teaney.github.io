Problem 3.   Entropy of Paramagnets

Consider a paramagnet interacting with a heat bath at temperature T consisting of N independent spins with N large. Let N↑ denote the number of up spins , and N↓ the number of down spins. A magnetic field B points in the z direction, and the spins want to align with the magnetic field. The energy levels of each spin are ∓μB⁢B, where the spin up states have lower energy -μB⁢B, and the spin down states have an energy of +μB⁢B. So, the spin down states have a higher energy than the spin up states by an amount Δ=2⁢μB⁢B . Since it is energetically favorable for the spin to be in the direction of B, it is the down spins that are excited. The mean number of excited atoms (spin down) is n¯=N¯↓/N . The total mean energy of the atoms is E¯=-μB⁢B⁢(N¯↑-N¯↓).

Problem 4.   Paramagnets from the Microcanonial Ensemble

Now we will work through the paramagnet problem in the micro-canonical ensemble. Recall that in the microcanonical ensemble we are supposed to directly count the number of configurations (states) with a given total fixed energy E. The system is closed, meaning it does not exchange heat with the environment. This counting procedure determines the entropy, and from there all else can be determined.

  1. (a)

    Describe the state of lowest possible energy (the ground state), and show that the energy of this state is -μB⁢B⁢N. Let’s define the excitation energy ℰ=E-(-N⁢μB⁢B), i.e. the energy above the ground state energy. Show that

    ℰN=n⁢Δ (5)
  2. (b)

    Show that the total number of configurations with excitation energy ℰ is

    Ω⁢(ℰ)=N!N↑!⁢N↓! (6)

    where N↑=N-N↓ and ℰ=N⁢n⁢Δ

  3. (c)

    Show that the entropy as a function of energy is

    S⁢(ℰ)=N⁢kB⁢[(1-n)⁢log⁡(1-n)-n⁢log⁡n] (7)
  4. (d)

    Using Eq. (7) show that the temperature of the system with a given ℰ is related to the mean number of down arrows

    Δk⁢T=ln⁡(1-nn) (8)
