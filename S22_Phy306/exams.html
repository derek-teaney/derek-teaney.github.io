Final Exam on 5/17 from 8:00--10:45 in classroom

Basic content

As before my intent will be to focuss on the mainstream, i.e. problems which were adequately treated in homework. The exam is cumulative but with a strong emphasis on material since after the midterm. In practice, this means that there won't be an ideal gas engine cycle. But, basic notions probabilitiy, flux, solid angles, integration over solid angle, partition functions, are all fair game.

The material since the midterm includes

  1. Thermodynamics and thermodyanic relations.
    • The van der Walls gas as an example that goes beyond the ideal gas.
  2. Partititon Functions of Simple Systems:
    • Ideal gas, perhaps with internal energy levels.
    • Quantum rotational partitions functions, and its classical approximation.
    • Paramagnetic systems, work, energy, entropy. Determining the magnetization, susceptibility
  3. Variable numbers of particles:
    • Simple models, e.g. Hemoglobin (see lecture). Problem
    • Ideal gas equilibrium, and the Saha Equation.
    • Fluctuations of particle number in an ideal gas
  4. Fermi and Bose distribution
    • Deriving the Fermi and Bose distributions, and Grand Potential.
    • The grand partitition function of photons
    • Energy and number per frequency interval, energy per wavelength interval
    • Density of modes
    • Entropy and free energy of a photon gas
    • Flux of photons through the hole
    • First quantum correction to a non-relativistic gas of fermions and bosons.
